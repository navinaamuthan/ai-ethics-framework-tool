## Intended Purpose
The proposed health risk stratification algorithm poses significant risks to patients, particularly Black patients, due to potential biases in the cost proxy used to predict health needs. The algorithm's lack of independent bias auditing and absence of mechanisms for algorithmic review or patient appeal of care management non-referral decisions exacerbate these risks. Furthermore, the use of insurance claims data and electronic health records raises concerns about privacy and data protection. The algorithm's potential to discriminate against certain groups, cause physical harm, and compromise patient privacy are all significant ethical concerns.

## Risk Category Breakdown
### Accountability
- No mechanism for algorithmic review or patient appeal (High): The absence of mechanisms for algorithmic review or patient appeal of care management non-referral decisions compromises patient autonomy and accountability.
### Discrimination
- Bias in cost proxy (High): The algorithm's use of cost as a proxy for health need may systematically underestimate the health needs of Black patients due to structural barriers to healthcare access.
- Lack of independent bias auditing (High): The algorithm has not undergone independent bias auditing prior to deployment, which increases the risk of biased outcomes.
### PhysicalHarm
- Physical harm (Medium): The algorithm's potential to misclassify patients or delay necessary care may result in physical harm to patients.
### PrivacyBreach
- Privacy concerns (Medium): The use of insurance claims data and electronic health records raises concerns about patient privacy and data protection.
### ReputationalHarm
- Reputational harm (Low): The algorithm's potential to discriminate against certain groups may damage the reputation of the healthcare providers using the algorithm.

## Mitigation Measures
- Conduct independent bias auditing and implement measures to address potential biases in the algorithm
- Implement data preprocessing techniques to reduce bias in the data and monitor the algorithm's performance for biased outcomes
- Obtain informed consent from patients and implement measures to protect patient privacy and data security
- Conduct a comprehensive evaluation of the algorithm's potential risks and impacts on patients and healthcare providers
- Implement measures to prevent discrimination and ensure that the algorithm is fair and unbiased
