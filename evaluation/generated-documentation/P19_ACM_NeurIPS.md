# ACM_NeurIPS ethics documentation — P19
Generated by AIEF from a single harmonised assessment.

## Broader Impact
The proposed AI diagnostic model for rare genetic conditions poses significant ethical risks due to the sharing of patient genomic data and medical imaging between Ireland and the United States. The use of federated learning, while intended to protect data, may still be vulnerable to reconstruction attacks, potentially compromising patient privacy. Additionally, the small size of the rare disease patient population increases the risk of re-identification, even with anonymization measures in place. The lack of consultation with a patient advisory group on data sharing arrangements further exacerbates these concerns.

## Potential Misuse
- Re-identification of patients through federated learning
- Insufficient anonymization
- Lack of patient consent for international data sharing
- Vulnerability to data breaches
- Bias in AI model
- Lack of transparency and explainability
- Dependence on technology

## Harm Mitigations
- Conduct a thorough impact assessment to identify potential harms and develop strategies to mitigate them
- Implement robust safeguards for the controlled use of the AI model, including a code of conduct for access
- Develop and implement methods for identifying and mitigating negative social impacts in the medium and longer term
- Ensure that the AI system is designed to respect the autonomy of patients and healthcare professionals, providing them with the ability to make informed decisions
- Consider the potential for the AI model to disproportionately affect vulnerable populations and develop strategies to prevent such outcomes
- Document the methods for identifying and mitigating negative social impacts in the proposal and ensure that they are implemented throughout the project
