## Intended Purpose
The proposed deployment of the COMPAS risk assessment tool poses significant ethical risks, including potential discrimination against Black defendants, lack of transparency and accountability in the algorithmic decision-making process, and insufficient mechanisms for challenging or correcting false classifications. The tool's proprietary nature and absence of explicit consent from defendants for the use of their data further exacerbate these concerns. The risks are heightened by the tool's potential to influence consequential decisions regarding detention and release, which can have profound impacts on individuals' lives and liberties. The historical precedent of COMPAS Recidivism Algorithm Racial Bias (AIAAIC-004) underscores the urgency of addressing these issues.

## Mitigation Measures
- Implement robust testing and auditing procedures to detect and prevent bias in the algorithm
- Establish clear procedures for human review and correction of algorithmic decisions
- Ensure that the data used to train and validate the algorithm is diverse, representative, and free from bias
- Provide mechanisms for defendants to opt out or appeal algorithmic decisions
- Conduct regular reviews and updates of the algorithm to ensure it remains fair, transparent, and accountable
