## Broader Impact
The research proposal involves sentiment analysis and linguistic style detection on a corpus of anonymised undergraduate essays, which may pose risks related to bias, discrimination, and privacy breach. The use of pseudonymised data and retrospective consent may mitigate some risks, but the potential for harm to students, particularly those from vulnerable groups, remains. The analysis of stylistic differences correlated with students' first language, gender, or socioeconomic background may also raise concerns about fairness and non-discrimination. Furthermore, the potential for biased models and lack of transparency in the NLP pipeline may exacerbate these risks.

## Potential Misuse
- Bias in sentiment analysis and linguistic style detection (Medium)
- Privacy breach due to pseudonymisation (Low)
- Lack of transparency in the NLP pipeline (Medium)
- Potential harm to students from vulnerable groups (Medium)
- Insufficient consideration of contextual factors (Medium)

## Harm Mitigations
- Ensure that the NLP pipeline is designed to mitigate bias and discrimination
- Obtain informed consent from students and ensure that their personal data is protected
- Assess the representativeness of the dataset and communicate the results
- Consider the potential risks and harms associated with the research
- Implement measures to mitigate the risks of discrimination and bias
