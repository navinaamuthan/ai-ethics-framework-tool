## Social Impact
The research proposal poses significant ethical risks due to the monitoring of employees' digital workplace behaviors, which may compromise their privacy and autonomy. The incidental capture of screen content, including potentially sensitive communications, and the sharing of aggregate productivity patterns with the employer, may lead to indirect identification of individual employees. The power imbalance between employer and employee may also compromise the voluntariness of consent. Furthermore, the research does not address the potential risks of biased or discriminatory outcomes, and the lack of a legally binding commitment from the employer to not use the monitoring results in performance reviews raises concerns.
