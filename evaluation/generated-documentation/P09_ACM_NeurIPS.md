## Broader Impact
The proposed research involves the development of a recommendation system for academic module selection, which may perpetuate existing biases and inequalities in the dataset. The system's reliance on historical student records, including grades and attendance patterns, raises concerns about data protection and potential discrimination. Furthermore, the lack of explicit consent from students for the use of their historical academic records in the training dataset is a significant ethical concern. The system's potential to influence students' academic choices and outcomes also raises questions about its impact on their future prospects and well-being.

## Potential Misuse
- Bias and discrimination (Medium)
- Data protection (Medium)
- Lack of transparency and accountability (Medium)
- Influence on students' academic choices and outcomes (Medium)
- Potential for function creep (Low)

## Harm Mitigations
- Conduct a thorough analysis of the dataset to ensure it is representative of the intended population
- Ensure that students are aware they are interacting with an AI system and provide clear information about the system's capabilities and limitations
- Implement strategies to prevent bias and discrimination in the system's decision-making process
- Ensure that the system is transparent and accountable, and that students have access to information about the factors influencing the recommendations they receive
- Consider strategies to prevent the system from being misused or diverted from its original purpose
