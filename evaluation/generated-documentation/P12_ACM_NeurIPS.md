## Broader Impact
The proposed research involves developing a machine learning model to optimise hospital bed allocation, which poses risks related to biased predictions, potential discrimination against vulnerable groups, and lack of clinical override mechanisms. The use of electronic health records, including sensitive information such as medical card status, raises concerns about data protection and privacy. The integration of the model's outputs into the hospital's electronic patient record system without a mechanism for clinical override documentation increases the risk of autonomous decision-making. The potential for biased predictions and lack of transparency in the decision-making process may lead to unintended consequences, such as unequal access to healthcare services.

## Potential Misuse
- Biased predictions
- Lack of clinical override mechanisms
- Data protection and privacy concerns
- Lack of transparency in decision-making process
- Potential for unequal access to healthcare services

## Harm Mitigations
- Conduct thorough testing and validation of the model
- Implement mechanisms for clinical override and documentation
- Ensure transparency in the decision-making process
- Implement data protection and privacy measures
- Conduct regular audits and assessments to ensure compliance with regulatory requirements
