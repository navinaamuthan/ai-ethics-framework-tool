# FRIA ethics documentation — P11
Generated by AIEF from a single harmonised assessment.

## Intended Purpose
The proposed automated essay grading system poses several ethical risks, including potential biases against non-native English speakers, lack of transparency in AI-assisted grading, and insufficient measures to address algorithmic errors. The system's sensitivity to grammatical patterns associated with non-native speakers may lead to systematically different scores, potentially discriminating against these students. Furthermore, the absence of an appeals process specifically addressing algorithmic grading errors may infringe upon students' right to effective remedy. The use of AI-assisted grading without informing students also raises concerns about transparency and accountability.

- Article 41: Good Administration
- Article 47: Right to Effective Remedy

## Mitigation Measures
- Implement measures to prevent bias in the grading algorithm
- Provide clear and transparent information about the automated essay grading system
- Establish an appeals process specifically addressing algorithmic grading errors
- Ensure that the training dataset is diverse and representative
- Regularly monitor and evaluate the performance of the automated essay grading system
