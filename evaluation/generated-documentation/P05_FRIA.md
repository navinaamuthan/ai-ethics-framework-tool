## Intended Purpose
The proposed deployment of a machine learning sepsis prediction algorithm across NHS England hospital trusts poses significant ethical risks, including potential discrimination against minority ethnic patient populations, privacy breaches, and physical harm due to inaccurate predictions. The algorithm's lack of demographic bias auditing and transparency in feature weighting and performance variance exacerbate these risks. Furthermore, the absence of a formal mechanism for clinicians to record or escalate concerns about demographic performance disparities raises concerns about accountability and fairness.

## Risk Category Breakdown
### Discrimination
- Discrimination against minority ethnic patient populations (High): The algorithm's training dataset has documented under-representation of Black, Asian, and minority ethnic patient populations, which may lead to biased predictions and unfair treatment.
- Lack of transparency in feature weighting and performance variance (Medium): The absence of transparency in feature weighting and performance variance may obscure biases and discriminatory patterns in the algorithm's predictions.
- Inadequate accountability and fairness mechanisms (Medium): The lack of a formal mechanism for clinicians to record or escalate concerns about demographic performance disparities raises concerns about accountability and fairness.
### PhysicalHarm
- Physical harm due to inaccurate predictions (High): Inaccurate predictions may lead to delayed or inappropriate treatment, resulting in physical harm or even death.
### PrivacyBreach
- Privacy breaches (Medium): The algorithm processes sensitive patient data, including physiological parameters and laboratory values, which may be vulnerable to unauthorised access or disclosure.

## Mitigation Measures
- Conduct demographic bias auditing and implement fairness mechanisms to ensure equal treatment of all patient populations
- Implement data preprocessing techniques to mitigate biases in the training dataset and ensure fair representation of all patient populations
- Ensure that patient data is anonymised and protected in accordance with relevant data protection regulations
- Conduct a thorough risk assessment and evaluation of the algorithm's potential impacts on patient outcomes and healthcare systems
- Implement transparency mechanisms to ensure that clinicians and patients are aware of the algorithm's predictions and limitations
