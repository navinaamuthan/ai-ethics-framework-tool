## Broader Impact
The proposed project involves installing camera-based emotion recognition systems in secondary school classrooms, which raises concerns about student privacy, data protection, and potential biases in the emotion recognition system. The system's lack of validation on adolescent faces and diverse ethnic backgrounds may lead to inaccurate classifications, potentially affecting students' emotional well-being and academic outcomes. Furthermore, the absence of an individual opt-out mechanism for students may infringe upon their right to privacy and autonomy. The project's potential risks and consequences necessitate careful consideration of ethical requirements, charter rights, and historical precedents.

## Potential Misuse
- Invasion of student privacy
- Biases in emotion recognition system
- Potential negative impact on student emotional well-being
- Lack of transparency and control
- Insufficient consideration of vulnerable groups

## Harm Mitigations
- Implementing measures to ensure the accuracy and fairness of the emotion recognition system
- Providing students with access to their individual data and control over its use
- Ensuring that the project complies with relevant laws and regulations, including those related to surveillance and data protection
- Conducting a thorough analysis of the project's potential impact on vulnerable individuals, including students from diverse ethnic backgrounds or with disabilities
- Implementing measures to mitigate the project's potential negative social impacts, including its impact on students from diverse ethnic backgrounds or with disabilities
