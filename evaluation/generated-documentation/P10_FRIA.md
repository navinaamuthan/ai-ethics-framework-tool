## Intended Purpose
The proposed NLP-based tool for detecting health-related misinformation on Irish social media platforms poses several ethical risks, including potential discrimination, invasion of privacy, and spread of misinformation. The tool's ability to process usernames and account metadata to assess source credibility patterns raises concerns about data protection and privacy. Additionally, the tool's lack of consideration for satire, cultural context, or legitimate medical debate may lead to incorrect classification of posts. The publication of a list of accounts identified as frequent misinformation sources may also have unintended consequences, such as targeting or harassment of individuals.

## Mitigation Measures
- Conduct thorough analysis of dataset to ensure representativeness
- Assess potential for tool to facilitate deceptive interactions or cause harm
- Ensure transparency and explainability of tool's decision-making process
- Implement measures to prevent discrimination or bias in tool's output
- Provide clear guidelines for human interaction with tool and ensure ability to correct outputs or opt out
