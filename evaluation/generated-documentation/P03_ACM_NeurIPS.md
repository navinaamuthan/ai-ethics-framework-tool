## Broader Impact
The proposed deployment of Amazon Rekognition for law enforcement agencies poses significant ethical risks, including potential misidentification of individuals, particularly people of color, and the lack of independent oversight mechanisms. The system's default confidence threshold of 80% has been shown to produce false positives, which could lead to wrongful arrests and convictions. Furthermore, the deployment of this technology raises concerns about mass surveillance, privacy, and the potential for biased decision-making. The absence of explicit consent from individuals whose faces are being matched against databases also raises concerns about the processing of special category data.

## Potential Misuse
- Misidentification of individuals
- Lack of independent oversight mechanisms
- Mass surveillance and privacy concerns
- Biased decision-making
- Processing of special category data without consent

## Harm Mitigations
- Conduct a thorough assessment of the system's potential biases and implement measures to mitigate them
- Establish an independent oversight mechanism to address false match consequences and ensure accountability
- Provide clear information to end-users about the system's capabilities and limitations
- Ensure that the system is designed and deployed in a way that contributes to society and human well-being
- Implement measures to prevent the system from being used to facilitate illegal activity or build upon existing illegal activities
