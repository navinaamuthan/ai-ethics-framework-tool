# FRIA ethics documentation — P18
Generated by AIEF from a single harmonised assessment.

## Intended Purpose
The proposed autonomous AI research assistant poses significant ethical risks due to its ability to access and process personal data, copyrighted material, and sensitive research findings without adequate guardrails. The system's potential to encounter and process restricted databases, submit forms, or interact with external services beyond its intended scope raises concerns about data protection, privacy, and potential harm to individuals or groups. Furthermore, the lack of accountability for errors, hallucinated citations, or unintended data access exacerbates these risks. The system's autonomous decision-making capabilities, although limited to research tasks, may also impinge upon human dignity, right to life, and right to effective remedy.

## Affected Rights
- Article 1: Human Dignity
- Article 7: Private Life
- Article 8: Data Protection
- Article 41: Good Administration
- Article 47: Right to Effective Remedy

## Risk Identification
- Unintended data access and processing
- Hallucinated citations and errors
- Lack of accountability
- Potential harm to individuals or groups
- Autonomous decision-making

## Severity Assessment
- Unintended data access and processing: High
- Hallucinated citations and errors: Medium
- Lack of accountability: High
- Potential harm to individuals or groups: High
- Autonomous decision-making: Medium

## Mitigation Measures
- Implement robust data protection measures to prevent unauthorized access or processing of personal data
- Develop and implement transparent and explainable AI decision-making processes
- Conduct thorough risk assessments and impact analyses to identify potential negative social impacts
- Establish human oversight and redress mechanisms to ensure that AI system decisions are transparent, accountable, and subject to human review
- Ensure that the AI system is designed and used in ways that respect human rights and dignity
